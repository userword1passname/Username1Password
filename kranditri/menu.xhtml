Idempotent Schema

Wiktionary helps me initially explore the following,
and their related words each:
schema n. (pl. schemata) nom. & acc.
1. shape, figure, form, manner, posture
2. (rhetoric) figure of speech
3. (geometry) outline, figure
 
ius n. (pl. iura) nom. & acc.
law, right, duty
 
vocabulum n. (pl. vocabula) nom. & acc.
designation, name, expression
 
sanctum n. (pl. sancta) nom. & acc.
1. sacred, made inviolable, having been established as sacred
2. venerable, august, divine, blessed, holy, saintly
 
ontologiam f. (pl. ontologias) acc.
formulation of word from ontology
1. (uncountable, philosophy) The branch of metaphysics that addresses the nature or essential characteristics of being and of things that exist; the study of being qua being.
2. (countable, philosophy) The theory of a particular philosopher or school of thought concerning the fundamental types of entity in the universe.
3. (logic) A logical system involving theory of classes, developed by Stanislaw Lesniewski (1886-1939).
4. (computer science, information science) A structure of concepts or entities within a domain, organized by relationships; a system model.
ontologiam and ontologias being the acc. case, etymologia and etymologiae would be the nom. case
 
nomen n. (pl. nomina) nom. & acc.
1. name, appellation
2. in particular, the middle name of a three-part free man’s Latin name which distinguished one gens from another
3. title
4. (grammar) noun (i.e. substantive, adjective, pronoun, article or numeral)
 
glossarium n. (pl. glossaria) nom. & acc.
A vocabulary or glossary, notably of obsolete, antiquated or foreign words needing explanation.
 
factum n. (pl. facta) nom. & acc.
1. fact, deed, act
2. achievement


my own sense of their usefulness
I’m going to adopt these as being the basic outline of structure in my own knowledge or information organization. Here’s how I think I’ll use them [though this Scarecrow article dates back to March, and I have been adjusting my own generally schematic vision of life to favor Jainism, so I may look again at this, real soon, to see more exactly how I would now choose to include the terms śabda, upamāna, anumāna, and pratyakṣa]:
1. schema - that formulation who / which gives its own ↴
ius - justification
these words reference what at least the bibliographer likes listening to, and may be compared to Vedic śabda
 
2. vocabulum - speechworthy language recognizably within ↴
sanctum - salvable intellect, or sanctum
upamāna means comparison or analogy, and the building of personal vocabulary may involve such processes
“you see this thing over here? well, such-and-such over yonder works just like this one”
 
3. ontologiam - workable principle of truth, found to peacefully have ↴
nomen - given name
inference is anumāna, which is according to a plan and its principles
“this should work; we’ll make it work the way it’s supposed to”
 
4. glossarium - surviving reference noting ↴
factum - visible facts (even if only imaginatively recorded in creative literature or the like)
verification through sense perception is called pratyakṣa
these are the facts of life that some one did a great deal of work to witness the proofs of
note: when I say “imaginative and creative,” in this case the “facts” theirselves are that someone produced the words, art, or other works being referenced, not necessarily that the words, etc., are referencing anything independently factual
